What actually happens when you use docuProof?

Under the hood it’s cryptographic hashing and anchoring on the Bitcoin blockchain. For you, it’s just three steps.

Step 1
Pick a file and a label

You choose a file and a short description. Your browser computes a one-way hash locally. The file itself never leaves your device.

Step 2
We anchor the hash on the Bitcoin blockchain

docuProof submits the hash through OpenTimestamps and anchors it onto the Bitcoin blockchain — a neutral, time-ordered public ledger.

Step 3
You keep the file, receipt, and certificate

You get a human-readable PDF certificate and an OpenTimestamps receipt. Kept with your original file, they form a durable proof of existence that can be independently verified later.

When should you timestamp something?

Any time you’d regret not being able to prove “this existed, exactly like this, on that date.”

Before you share
Drafts, decks, designs, research notes — anything that could later be reused or disputed.
Before you agree
Scopes of work, pricing sheets, policies and terms that could quietly change.
Before there’s a dispute
Photos, logs, incident reports — evidence that will matter only if something goes wrong.
