How docuProof works (without the jargon)

Under the hood it’s hashes, calendars and the Bitcoin blockchain. On your side it’s three simple steps.

Step 1
You choose a file and a label

In your browser, you pick a file and a display name. docuProof computes a one-way hash locally. We never see or store the file.

Step 2
We queue a proof on the Bitcoin blockchain

The hash is submitted through OpenTimestamps and batched for anchoring on the Bitcoin blockchain. You don’t need to run any infrastructure.

Step 3
You keep a receipt and a certificate

You receive a PDF certificate and an OpenTimestamps receipt. Later, anyone can independently confirm that your hash was committed before a specific block on the Bitcoin blockchain.

Common questions

A few quick answers. The full FAQ and pricing live on the Start page.

Do you ever upload or keep my file?

No. The hash is computed locally in your browser. docuProof only sees the hash and the metadata you choose to send (like a file name or display name).

Is this a legal notary service?

No. docuProof is a technical proof-of-existence tool, not a legal notary. It strengthens your evidence with a public, independently verifiable timestamp on the Bitcoin blockchain. How that is treated legally depends on your context.

Why the Bitcoin blockchain specifically?

The Bitcoin blockchain is globally replicated, hard to rewrite, and optimized for ordered, time-linked transactions. That makes it a strong base layer for timestamping — independent of any single company, including us.

What if docuProof goes away?

Your original file plus your OpenTimestamps receipt are enough. You can verify them using open-source tools against the Bitcoin blockchain without going through docuProof.
