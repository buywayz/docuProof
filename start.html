FAQ

Is this a legal notary service? ⌄

No. docuProof creates a cryptographic proof-of-existence, not a notarization. It verifies that your file existed at a specific time and has not changed since.

Does my file get uploaded or saved anywhere? ⌄

No. Your file never leaves your device. We hash it locally in your browser and only submit the SHA-256 fingerprint for blockchain anchoring.

What exactly gets stored on Bitcoin? ⌄

Only your file’s cryptographic fingerprint (a 64-character SHA-256 digest). It cannot be reversed or used to reconstruct the file. Your document’s contents always remain private.

How long does blockchain anchoring take? ⌄

Your certificate and Proof ID are issued instantly. Bitcoin anchoring depends on network activity and occurs asynchronously. Your certificate includes a link to live status and confirmations.

What should I save after generating a proof? ⌄

Save your original file and the PDF certificate you receive. Together they allow anyone to independently verify your proof at any time — with or without docuProof.

What types of files can I timestamp? ⌄

Almost anything: PDFs, images, videos, text files, manuscripts, contracts, research notes, artwork, audio files, and more. If it can be hashed, it can be proven.

Why is Bitcoin used instead of another blockchain? ⌄

Bitcoin provides the most secure, neutral, globally replicated ledger for proof-of-existence. Its immutability and longevity make it the preferred chain for long-term evidence.

Can I verify my proof without docuProof? ⌄

Yes. Your certificate includes everything needed to verify independently using OpenTimestamps and your original file. docuProof is not a point of failure.
