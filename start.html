FAQ

Is this a legal notary? ⌄

No. docuProof provides a blockchain timestamp (proof of existence), not a notarial act. It proves a file existed, unchanged, at a specific time.

Does my document get uploaded or stored? ⌄

No. Hashing happens locally in your browser; only the SHA-256 fingerprint is sent for anchoring. We never collect or store your document.

How long does anchoring take? ⌄

Fingerprints are batched and written to Bitcoin. Your proof receipt and ID are available immediately; blockchain confirmations may take time depending on network conditions. Your certificate explains how to check status and confirmations.

What should I save after I create a timestamp? ⌄

Keep your original file (e.g., contract.pdf) and its proof bundle (e.g., contract.pdf.ots plus an optional sha256.txt). Store them together so future verification is trivial.

Can I timestamp plain text or notes? ⌄

Yes. You can timestamp files or raw text. For text, we hash your input on-device and anchor that fingerprint in the same way.

Which blockchain do you use? ⌄

docuProof anchors to the Bitcoin blockchain for maximum security and verifiability. We prioritize permanence and censorship-resistance over speed-only networks.

What happens if I typed the wrong file or need a refund? ⌄

If you made a purchase error, contact us at support@docuproof.io within 7 days. We’ll review and help where possible. Note: anchoring transactions cannot be reversed once confirmed on-chain.

What if Bitcoin fees spike? ⌄

We batch proofs to keep costs predictable. In rare fee spikes, anchoring may take longer, but your Proof ID and certificate remain valid and verifiable.
